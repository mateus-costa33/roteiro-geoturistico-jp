Hydrography - Introduction

Water, as an element of geodiversity and a geopatrimonial element, will be approached through two features: river aspects and freshwater sources . Whenever possible, an attempt will be made to correlate these aspects with geomorphology, geology, tectonics and the environmental problems involved with anthropization, serving as a basis for later analyzing them through a heritage view, aiming to insert them in the geotourism tour. Thus, the details of these features will occur in the case of those that were selected as a Place of Interest, being present in the inventory of chapter 8.

Field trips were carried out between February and May 2016 on the coast of the study area, aiming at in situ observation of phenomena related to geodiversity, in addition to aerophotogrammetric analysis and orbital images and bibliographic research, relatively scarce for the subject in question, as well as the sources inserted in the Historic Center, with the subsequent purpose of valuation for geopatrimonial purposes.

Organizing the area from the point of view of hydrographic basins, according to the classification proposed by the National Department of Water and Electricity (DNAEE), the research area is totally inserted in the Hydrographic Basin of the Northeast Atlantic (IBIAPINA et al., 1999). Thinking on a state scale, in turn, the proposal of the Executive Agency for Water Management of the State of Paraíba (AESA) was used. The project area is drained by three larger basins: lower course of the Paraíba Basin; eastern portion of the Gramame River Basin; and eastern portion of the Abiaí River Basin, in addition to some adjacent microbasins.

In addition to these basins, some water courses form micro-basins that flow into the Atlantic Ocean and that were inserted in the basins previously mentioned. Following the concept of hydrographic basin 1 , we chose to dismember them from these basins and group them into two secondary basins, here called Secondary Basins of João Pessoa and Secondary Basins of the South Paraiba Coast , separated by the Rio Gramame Basin, as shown in figure 1. Paraíba River Basin - low course

The Paraíba River, also known as the Paraíba do Norte River, is the main river in the homonymous basin and the most extensive river entirely in Paraíba. The Paraíba River Basin is the second largest basin in Paraíba, lower than the Piranhas River Basin.

It has an area of ​​about 20 thousand Km 2 , bathing 38% of the State's area, with coordinates 6º51'47 ”- 8º18’12” of lat. South and 34º47’37 ”- 37º21’22” long. West. It is composed of the sub-basin of the Taperoá River and, geographically, subdivided into High, Medium and Low Course. It is its low course that bathes the municipalities of João Pessoa and Cabedelo, inserted in the area of ​​this project. Thus, because it also bathes the municipality of Campina Grande, second municipality in the state, this basin has the highest demographic density in the state, inserting more than half of the population of Paraíba2.

The Paraíba River is the longest river in the state of Paraiba, originating in the Serra de Jabitacá, municipality of Monteiro, at over 1,000 meters of altitude, in one of the driest areas of the country, belonging to the 'Polígono das Secas', in the Cariri Paraiba micro-region (LACERDA, 2003). To reach its mouth, which limits the municipalities of Cabedelo, João Pessoa, Santa Rita, Bayeux and Lucena, it travels 380 km and several different geomorphological units and climates, in addition to three mesoregions3.

Along this route, the Paraíba River suffers from several environmental and man-made problems. Due to the fact that it was born in an arid region, a good part of the basin has an intermittent character, which forced several governmental administrations to build dams and dams, such as the Epitácio Pessoa Weir, in Boqueirão, responsible for supplying the municipality of Campina Grade, the second largest urban center of the State (CUNHA, 1998). However, according to AESA (2009), the water storage capacity in these dams is over one billion m 3 , whose dams alter their river dynamics, resulting in a greater spacing of the floods along its course (SILVA, 2003), floods that have reached its estuary since the first records of colonial occupation began to be documented, that is, in the 17th century.

Another problem that occurs in its medium and high course and that ends up affecting its estuary is the silting process, the result of inadequate agricultural practices, associated with the cultivation of sugar cane and the intense deforestation of its riparian forests. Large volumes of sediments reach the low course of the river, much of which comes from the erosion of nearby hillsides, and deposit as the river capacity decreases. The result is the accumulation of these sediments, forming sandbanks, islets, reducing the width and depth of the river and making its waters more turbid.

The Paraíba River estuary is about 22 km from its mouth in Cabedelo to the Sanhauá River bridge in João Pessoa. Its main tributaries on the left bank are the Portinho, Tiriri, Ribeira and Guia rivers, all outside the project area. The tributaries of the right bank, already in the area of ​​work, are the Rio Sanhauá-Marés, Paroeira and Mandacaru (SUDENE, 1974). The Jaguaribe River, which forms one of the main urban microbasins in João Pessoa, flows into the Mandacarú River, a tributary of the right bank of the Paraíba River and, therefore, will be considered a sub-basin of the Paraíba Basin in its low course.

Sub-basin of the Marés-Sanhauá River

When Rio do Meio and Marés meet, the Sanhauá River is formed. This river touches the lower city of João Pessoa, in the historic center, for about 8 km, while molding the boards to the east, serving as a natural border, along with the Marés River, between João Pessoa and Bayeux, to the west, flowing into Rio Paraíba (COSTA, 2009). His main problem was that he received, between 1957 and 2003, effluents from Roger's dump , located close to his low course. It corresponds to an area with a relatively poor drainage network, despite the denudational notches of the Rio Marés are relatively intense, resulting in steep slopes (FURRIER, 2007).

The handshake between the leader Piragibe and João Tavares, representing the Portuguese Crown, which sealed the peace agreement and later provided the foundation of the city of Nossa Senhora das Neves, took place on the banks of the Sanhauá River, hence its importance only ecological-environmental, but historical.

In general, both rivers are relatively linear, with a predominantly SW-NW flow direction and shallow escarpments. Barbosa and Furrier (2012) performed a morphological and morphometric evaluation in Rio Marés, concluding that the configuration of the relief pointed to evidence of neotectonic activities. Both rivers are characterized as of the subsequent type, corroborated by Andrades Filho (2010), who studied the sub-basin using remote sensing techniques, suggesting that this region was affected by neotectonic failures, of Quaternary age.
